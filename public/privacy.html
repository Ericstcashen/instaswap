Swaply Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 5, 2026

Swaply helps two people exchange Instagram usernames quickly by scanning a code. This policy explains exactly what we collect and what we do with it. In short: we collect the minimum needed to make a swap work, and we never sell your data or show ads.

What we collect

  • Your Instagram username — the handle you type in. That's it. We never ask for your Instagram password and you never log into Instagram through Swaply.
  • Swaps you make — when you swap with someone, we record that your username was shared with the specific person (the "host") whose code you used, along with the time.
  • On your device only — your own username is remembered locally on your phone (browser storage) so you don't have to retype it. This stays on your device.

We do not collect your name, email, phone number, location, contacts, or any advertising identifiers. There is no analytics or third-party tracking in the app.

How your information is used

  • To deliver the username you chose to share to the person you chose to share it with.
  • To show a host the list of usernames that have swapped with their code, so they can follow back.

Sharing a username is always something you initiate by scanning a code and tapping Swap.

Who can see your username

Only the specific host whose code you scan. Usernames are not public, not searchable, and not shared with any other users or third parties.

Where data is stored

Swap records are stored on our own server infrastructure and are not shared with advertisers or data brokers. We use reasonable measures to protect this data.

Deleting your data

To have your username and swap records removed, email us and we'll delete them.

Children

Swaply is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect their data.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date at the top of this page.
